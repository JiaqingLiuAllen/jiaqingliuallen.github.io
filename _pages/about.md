I'm an ECE Master's student working at UW's <a href='https://ubicomplab.cs.washington.edu/'>Ubiquitous Computing Lab</a>, advised by <a href='https://people.ece.uw.edu/patel_shwetak/'>Shwetak Patel</a>. My current research focuses on creating novel wearable devices for continuous, passive physiological sensing. I aim to develop ubiquitous, end-to-end sensing systems that enhance current health monitoring methods and improve people's well-being.

Before joining UW, I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Biomedical Engineering from Southwest Jiaotong University, advised by <a href='https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=3X-4YmUAAAAJ&view_op=list_works'>Weili Deng</a>. During my time at the Wearable Electronics Lab, I specialized in designing and fabricating on-body human-machine interfaces based on flexible electronic materials and devices.

With an interdisciplinary background in Electrical Engineering, Biomedical Engineering, and Materials Science, I am well-equipped to bridge the gaps among embedded computing systems, medical principles, and human-computer interfaces to help people better understand their surroundings and own physiological signals.
