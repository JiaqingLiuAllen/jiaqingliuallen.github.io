I'm an ECE Master's student working at UW <a href='https://ubicomplab.cs.washington.edu/'>Ubicomp Lab</a>, advised by Dr. <a href='https://people.ece.uw.edu/patel_shwetak/'>Shwetak Patel</a>. My research focuses on developing novel end-to-end computing systems for continuous, passive health monitoring. Specifically, I aim to integrate health sensing seamlessly into everyday objects, such as wearables and smartphones, to create new opportunities for accessible, personalized healthcare.

Before joining UW, I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Biomedical Engineering from Southwest Jiaotong University, advised by Dr. <a href='https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=3X-4YmUAAAAJ&view_op=list_works'>Weili Deng</a>. At the Wearable Electronics Lab, I specialized in designing and fabricating on-body human-machine interfaces using flexible electronics.

With an interdisciplinary background spanning ECE, Biomedical Engineering, and Materials Science, I am uniquely positioned to bridge gaps between embedded systems, medical principles, edge ML, and physical user interfaces. My full-stack skill set in hardware design, firmware development, and sensor data analysis enables me to drive innovations in both device form factors and personalized algorithms.
